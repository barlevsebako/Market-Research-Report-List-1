<p><p>Model train markets can primarily be categorized into two systems: DC current two-rails and AC current three-rails. The DC current two-rails system uses two tracks for power supply and typically offers simpler wiring and control, making it popular among beginners and hobbyists. In contrast, the AC current three-rails system employs a third rail for power, providing more reliable operation and enabling features such as multiple trains on the same track without interference, appealing to seasoned modelers and complex layouts.</p></p>
