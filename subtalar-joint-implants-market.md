<p><p>The subtalar joint implants market is divided into cemented and cementless types. Cemented implants utilize bone cement to secure the device, providing stability and effective load distribution, often preferred in patients with quality bone. In contrast, cementless implants rely on biological fixation through bone growth into the implant surface, promoting longer-term integration. Each type suits different patient needs based on factors like bone quality, age, and activity level, impacting overall treatment outcomes and recovery processes.</p></p>
